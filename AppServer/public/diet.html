Diet

  • Why?

    • A healthy diet plays a significant role in promoting longevity by reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and certain cancers. Consuming a variety of nutrient-rich foods helps maintain healthy skin, teeth, and eyes, while also supporting muscle strength and boosting the immune system to protect against illnesses.

    In addition to these benefits, a balanced diet strengthens bones, which is crucial for preventing osteoporosis and fractures. It also supports healthy pregnancies and breastfeeding by providing essential nutrients for both mother and baby. Furthermore, a healthy diet aids in digestive health by ensuring proper nutrient absorption and promoting regular bowel movements. Maintaining a balanced diet helps achieve and sustain a healthy weight, which is vital for overall well-being and long-term health.

  • Gaining Weight/Bulking

    • When bulking, eating at a caloric surplus is essential to promote muscle growth. A caloric surplus puts your body in an anabolic state, which supports the building of new tissue, including muscle and bone. However, it’s important to control your surplus by consuming slightly more than your maintenance calorie needs. This approach helps ensure gradual increases in strength and weight with minimal fat gain.

    A well-rounded bulking diet should include moderate amounts of protein and fat, along with a substantial intake of carbohydrates. Carbs are crucial for glycogen production, which fuels heavy lifting sessions. The insulin response from carbs also aids in muscle growth. While protein is important, you don’t need as much as commonly believed; about 1.3-1.8g of protein per kilogram of body weight is sufficient for muscle gain. Fat intake should be moderate to maintain hormonal balance. Ideal foods for bulking include lean meats, eggs, dairy, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, and starchy vegetables like sweet potatoes and rice. Avoid "dirty bulking" by focusing on nutrient-dense foods, which helps minimize excess fat gain and supports overall health.

  • Maintaining Weight

    • Maintaining your current weight requires consuming roughly the same number of calories as you burn, which is known as your maintenance calorie level. It’s important to find this balance, as eating too much can lead to weight gain, while eating too little can cause weight loss. To maintain weight, focus on eating a variety of nutrient-dense foods in portions that align with your energy needs.

    Your diet should include a balance of protein, fats, and carbohydrates to support overall health and well-being. Protein is essential for muscle maintenance, while fats help regulate hormones and support cell function. Carbs provide energy, particularly for physical activity. Healthy sources of protein include lean meats, fish, eggs, beans, and dairy, while healthy fats come from avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil. Whole grains, vegetables, and fruits provide complex carbohydrates and important vitamins and minerals. To avoid weight fluctuation, pay attention to portion sizes and avoid overeating, while staying active to maintain your calorie burn.

  • Losing Weight/Cutting

    • When cutting, the goal is to eat at a caloric deficit, consuming fewer calories than your body burns. This puts you in a "catabolic" state, where tissue is broken down for energy. Keep your calorie intake slightly below maintenance but avoid extreme cuts. Gradual, sustainable changes yield the best long-term results, while ensuring you get all the necessary nutrients for overall health. Focus on whole foods like lean proteins (chicken, turkey, fish), vegetables, fruits, and whole grains to stay nourished and satisfied.

    To aid in cutting, incorporate exercises that raise your heart rate and burn calories, such as cardio or HIIT. Reducing rest times between sets and adding supersets can also increase calorie burn. Increasing NEAT (non-exercise activity thermogenesis)—calories burned through daily tasks—can support weight loss. Protein is crucial for muscle preservation during a cut, aiming for about 1.8g per kg of body weight. Continue strength training to maintain muscle, and include foods like eggs, tofu, Greek yogurt, and legumes. While muscle gains may be limited, these habits help retain lean mass.

  • Pros

    • A heart-healthy diet is essential for preventing cardiovascular disease (CVD), which is the leading cause of death in the U.S. Following the DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet, which includes plenty of vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and low-fat dairy, can help manage blood pressure and reduce heart disease risk. Limiting saturated fats, added sugars, and sodium while increasing potassium, magnesium, and calcium is key. High-fiber foods like beans and oats also improve cholesterol and heart health.

    A balanced diet can also lower cancer risks by incorporating a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and beans. For mental health, the Mediterranean diet has been linked to improved mood. Probiotic-rich foods like yogurt and kimchi support gut health, while nutrient-dense foods such as omega-3s and B vitamins help boost brain function and memory. Maintaining a healthy weight through a balanced diet can reduce the risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and other chronic conditions, while also improving sleep quality.
