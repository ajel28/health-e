04

Traps

  • Muscles
    • Upper (Superior) Section: Elevates the scapula (shoulder shrugging) and assists in upward rotation for overhead movements. Originates from the skull and cervical spine, inserting on the clavicle and acromion.
    • Middle Section: Retracts the scapula (pulling it toward the spine) to stabilize the shoulder. Originates from upper thoracic vertebrae and inserts on the scapular spine.
    • Lower (Inferior) Section: Depresses and assists in upward rotation of the scapula, crucial for arm elevation. Originates from lower thoracic vertebrae and inserts at the scapular spine base.
    • Shoulder Shrugs

      • 3x10-12 (3 sets, 10-12 repitions)

      Shoulder shrugs are a resistance exercise typically done in the gym or at home with weights. The key focus of the shoulder shrug exercise is to isolate the trapezius muscle, developing size and strength for improved aesthetics and performance.

      Despite targeting the upper trapezius muscle, shoulder shrugs also help develop the shoulders and neck. A relatively self-explanatory exercise, the shoulder shrug is essentially 'shrugging' your shoulders.

    • Rack Pulls

      • 3x10-12 (3 sets, 10-12 repitions)

      The rack pull, is a deadlift variation in which a loaded barbell is set up on the supports of a power rack, usually just above or below the knees, and lifted by gripping the bar and extending the hips to full lockout.

      This high-intensity deadlift alternative efficiently builds pulling strength, which transfers well to a wide range of athletics or even simply to improving your deadlift max. Additionally, the rack pull places significant emphasis on the trapezius muscles, particularly during the lockout phase. The shorter range of motion allows for heavier loads, which create a strong isometric contraction in the traps as they stabilize the shoulders and assist in pulling the bar upward. Over time, this helps to develop upper back thickness and overall trap strength, essential for lifting performance and injury prevention.
