06

Bicep

  • Muscles
    • Short Head: This part of the biceps originates from the coracoid process of the scapula (shoulder blade). It contributes to elbow flexion and assists with shoulder stability during arm movements.
    • Long Head: The long head begins at the supraglenoid tubercle, a small bump just above the shoulder joint. It plays a key role in forearm supination (rotating the palm up) and helps stabilize the shoulder during overhead motions
    • Brachialis: Located underneath the biceps brachii, this muscle is the primary elbow flexor. It doesn’t assist in forearm rotation but is crucial for lifting objects when the palm is facing downward.
    • Hammer Curls

      • 3x8-10 (3 sets, 8-10 repitions)

      Hammer curls, also known as dumbbell hammer curls or neutral grip dumbbell curls, is a strength training exercise that specifically targets your biceps and your forearms. A hammer curl varies from a traditional bicep curl because you use a neutral grip with your palms facing each other during the full range of motion.

      This neutral grip shifts some of the focus onto the brachialis, a muscle underneath the biceps, and the brachioradialis, a prominent forearm muscle, contributing to greater overall arm strength and size. Hammer curls are particularly beneficial for enhancing grip strength and improving the functionality of the arms for daily activities and athletic performance. They are a versatile exercise, suitable for beginners and advanced lifters, and can be performed with dumbbells, resistance bands, or a cable machine to add variety to your workout routine.

    • Incline Dumbbell Curls

      • 3x10-12 (3 sets, 10-12 repitions)

      The incline bicep curl trains the biceps behind the body, creating a greater range of motion than the standard bicep curl and putting more emphasis on the long head of the bicep. The incline bicep curl uses an incline bench and requires the performer to sit down and extend the arms so that they hang behind the body. Keeping the upper body, shoulders and upper arms in a strict position, the biceps are challenged to raise the dumbbells to shoulder height and back down again.

      By having the load behind the body, it may feel as though the beginning of the exercise is the most challenging, however the most difficult portion is during the mid-range. The use of the incline bench further allows for the muscles to be trained through a greater range of motion and the varying angle makes for a well-rounded bicep regime.

    • Bicep Curls

      • 3x8-10 (3 sets, 8-10 repitions)

      The bicep curl is a foundational exercise that targets the biceps, helping to build strength and definition in the upper arm. This versatile movement can be performed with dumbbells, barbells, resistance bands, or cables, making it accessible to all fitness levels.

      Bicep curls are highly effective for building strength, as they allow you to focus on progressively overloading the biceps by increasing the resistance over time. By challenging the muscle with heavier weights while maintaining proper form, you can improve overall arm strength and functionality. Strength-focused bicep curls also help stabilize your elbows and enhance your ability to perform pulling movements in other exercises, such as rows, chin-ups, and deadlifts.
